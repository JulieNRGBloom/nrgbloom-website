Investor Insights

land Acquisition

March 25, 2024
We have acquired land in a small community in Bayelsa State, Nigeria, which is supplied with 3MW of power from natural gas sourced directly from a nearby oil and gas company. Utilizing this gas, which would otherwise be flared, as our energy supply benefits the community by creating an income stream and provides us with a source of affordable power. This sustainable approach strengthens local economic development while supporting our operational needs.

Due Diligence

May 19, 2024 - June 3, 2024
Makir visited Nigeria to perform our due diligence, meeting with the law firm (Famsville) and lawyers who will represent us to ensure all our actions are legally sound.

After an extensive and meticulous due diligence process, our legal team has verified that both the land and the landlord meet all necessary compliance requirements, assuring us that everything is in perfect order.
Makir met with the community leaders and visited our acquired land, from where you can see the gas flaring site that supplies natural gas to power our operations.
Makir met with our local Nigerian partner who brokered the power purchase agreement and land lease deal, and our Nigerian contractors.

Land Excavation

June 14, 2024
Our contractors in Nigeria have completed the land excavation, and our engineer has conducted an on-site visit to the community to meticulously plan the placement of all infrastructure elements.

Signed service agreement with contractors

August 7, 2024
After discussions with our legal team at Famsville and contractors, both parties reached an agreement on the terms and conditions of the contractor's services. We provided funding for the purchase of transformers, construction materials for the container, and the transportation costs for all items.

Civil Works achieved

August 28, 2024
Our contractor's engineer completed the civil works, which included constructing a base for the transformer and six reinforced pillars for the containers. This structure ensures protection of our infrastructure from water damage.

Second visit to the community

September 3, 2024
Three members of our team—Makir, Jordane, and Gus—visited our site and brought gifts for the local children in the community.

Transformer and Container Material Reach our Land

September 4, 2024
After a lengthy journey from Lagos, our materials finally arrived in Bayelsa State, where they needed to be transported by boat to our land in the off-grid community. Despite some challenges along the way, the local community demonstrated incredible determination, pushing our 2-ton container by hand all the way to the site, proving that where there's a will, there's a way.

Transforming the shipping container into a mining container

September 10, 2024
On-site, our contractors began converting the shipping container we purchased into a Bitcoin mining container. They cut sections of the container walls to create ventilation pathways and installed racks where we will later place our equipment.

Placing transformer on the base and the container on the pillars

September 18, 2024
The entire community came together to lift the container by hand onto the pillars. Once again, they showed that nothing would stand in their way when it comes to helping us bring this project to life.

Building Security house, Fence, and finishing touches to the container

October 2024
After setting up the container and adding the finishing touches (racks, ventilation openings, networking equipment, and connecting the transformer), we shifted our attention to security. We built a security house so guards can stay on site 24/7, along with fencing around the site.

Installing ASIC Miners

January 2025
At the end of January 2025, we received and plugged in our first miners.

TON Visit

February 2025
In February we welcomed members of TON Infrastructure, a Canadian company, on our site to visit our operations in light of partnering up with them for future expansion.

Closing expansion deal

May 2025
In May 2025, two members of our team (Julie and Makir) went to Edmonton to meet with a TON Infrastructure to finalize and secure the expansion deal to build out the full 5MW. Phase 1, expanding to 1MW and installing newer ASICs in our current container, kicked off at the end of May 2025, with the mining container shipped from China and the ASICs coming from Canada.

Civil works for phase 1 expansion deal

June - August 2025
While the equipment and infrastructure were being shipped, we began planning the setup of the 1MW expansion. Land was leased and cleared, and the foundations for the container and transformer were set.

equipment ready to deploy

November 2025
After a long boat ride, both containers (mining container and container with the equipment) arrived at the port of Lagos. From there they were transported to the south of Nigeria where there are ready to be loaded on the barge.
